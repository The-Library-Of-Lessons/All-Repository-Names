The Foundation

What Is It?

A habit is a routine or behavior that is performed regularly and, in many cases, automatically. It is the brain's way of conserving energy by turning repetitive tasks into neural loops consisting of a cue, a craving, a response, and a reward.

The Necessity

Why It Matters

🌱

Compound Growth

Small 1% improvements every day result in being 37 times better by the end of the year through the power of compounding.

🧠

Cognitive Ease

By automating basic behaviors, you free up mental bandwidth for complex problem-solving and creative thinking.

🎯

Identity Shift

True behavior change is identity change. Every action you take is a vote for the type of person you wish to become.

⚡

Predictable Success

You do not rise to the level of your goals; you fall to the level of your systems. Habits are the building blocks of systems.

The Skills

The Pillars of Mastery

The Habit Loop

Understanding the Cue, Craving, Response, and Reward cycle is essential for manipulating and mastering any behavior.

Environment Design

Make good habits obvious and bad habits invisible. Your environment is a stronger driver of behavior than willpower.

The 2-Minute Rule

When you start a new habit, it should take less than two minutes to do. Optimize for the starting line, not the finish line.

Case Studies

British Cycling — Marginal Gains
Case Study
›

Sir Dave Brailsford transformed British cycling by looking for 1% improvements in everything from tire grip to the pillows athletes slept on.

The Lesson: Aggregating small, almost imperceptible improvements across many areas leads to radical transformation over time.

💬
Jerry Seinfeld — Don't Break the Chain
Case Study
›

Seinfeld used a wall calendar and a red marker to mark every day he wrote jokes. His only goal was to not break the chain of red X's.

The Lesson: Visual progress tracking provides immediate rewards and reinforces the identity of someone who shows up every day.

The Big Picture

Summary of All Lessons

L01

Make it Obvious. Use implementation intentions: 'I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION].' Explicit plans increase follow-through.

L02

Use Habit Stacking. Pair a new habit with a current one: 'After I [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT].' Leverage existing neural pathways.

L03

Optimize your Environment. Place the cues for good habits in front of you and hide the triggers for bad habits. Design for the path of least resistance.

L04

Make it Attractive. Use temptation bundling. Pair an action you need to do with an action you want to do to create a dopamine spike.

L05

Make it Easy. Reduce friction for good habits and increase friction for bad ones. Make it so easy you can't say no.

L06

Master the Decisive Moment. Identify the small choices that deliver outsized impact, like putting on your running shoes or opening your book.

L07

Make it Satisfying. The brain prioritizes immediate rewards over delayed ones. Use a habit tracker to make the progress itself feel rewarding.

L08

Never Miss Twice. Missing once is an accident. Missing twice is the start of a new habit. Get back on track immediately.

L09

Focus on Systems, Not Goals. Goals are about the results you want to achieve. Systems are about the processes that lead to those results.

L10

Embrace Identity-Based Habits. Instead of saying 'I want to lose weight,' say 'I am the type of person who never misses a workout.'

"The quality of our lives often depends on the quality of our habits. We are what we repeatedly do."
